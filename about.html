About Me

I'm Oliver — an ML engineer and researcher finishing my B.S. in Computer Science at Appalachian State University this May, with a minor in Mathematics and a Data Science certificate. I work on production ML systems and controlled empirical research, and my focus sits at the intersection of multi-task learning, reinforcement learning theory, and NLP / language modeling.

I'm currently an ML Engineer & Researcher at a stealth, pre-launch startup, which I joined as a senior-year intern after my capstone work became the initial production system and is now being transitioned into a full-time role post-graduation. Day-to-day I work directly with the founder and one other engineer on nearly all ML architecture and research decisions ahead of launch.

What drives me is understanding how models actually learn — particularly through attention mechanisms — and how decisions around architecture, training, and data shape performance, generalization, and interpretability. I like working close to the math, moving between theory and implementation, and letting experimental results pick the next step.

What I'm Working On

At the startup, I'm the core ML engineer on a 10-person pre-launch team (9 engineers + founder, advised by an external board). I shipped a production attribute and unit harmonization system — SBERT embeddings over 125K+ product attributes, cosine-similarity retrieval, GPU-accelerated batch inference, and confidence + top-k match evidence surfaced for output explainability. I also designed a schema-agnostic ingestion layer that auto-detects multiple JSON formats and normalizes them into a canonical attribute representation, plus unit-harmonization logic across physical, electrical, and thermal dimensions that teammates now consume for downstream visualizations and a browser extension.

On top of that I built a real-time ML operations dashboard in FastAPI + WebSockets, with batch upload, step-level event streaming, and grouped downloadable outputs for debugging and stakeholder demos. I'm also prototyping a second system — a multi-agent harmonization and product-data scraping pipeline built with Claude Agent Skills orchestrated through Obsidian — and presenting architecture deep-dives to the company's advisory board (specifics under NDA).

On the research side, my main project is LexiMind — a 272M-parameter encoder-decoder transformer I implemented from scratch (Pre-LN, RMSNorm, T5 relative position bias, FlashAttention, gated-GELU FFN, KV-cache) with explicit FLAN-T5-base weight mapping, jointly trained on abstractive summarization, topic classification, and multi-label emotion detection across literary and academic text. I diagnosed negative transfer in naive MTL and designed two targeted interventions — learned attention pooling for classification heads and temperature-based task sampling — improving emotion-detection sample-averaged F1 from 0.199 → 0.352 (+77%) and surpassing the single-task baseline. The shared-encoder MTL system also beats a BERT-base topic-classification specialist (85.7% vs. 83.1%). I'm targeting an arXiv preprint and EMNLP 2026 submission, with 5-seed validation, bootstrap CIs, gradient-conflict diagnostics, and MLflow tracking already in place.

Alongside that I've built an F1 race outcome predictor using classical ML methods and careful feature engineering, and PlayAxis — a full-stack sports platform integrating multiple external APIs with a FastAPI backend and PostgreSQL. These projects sharpened how I reason about real-world datasets, evaluation metrics, and end-to-end system design.
