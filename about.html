About

ML engineer and researcher.

Production ML systems with the research discipline to back them up.

I'm Oliver — an ML engineer and researcher based in Boone, NC. I'm finishing a B.S. in Computer Science at Appalachian State this May with a minor in Mathematics and a Data Science certificate, and I'm transitioning into a full-time ML role at the stealth, pre-launch startup I've been working with since my senior year. My research interests sit at the intersection of multi-task learning, reinforcement learning theory, and NLP / language modeling.

I joined the startup as a senior-year intern after my capstone — an ML harmonization system and real-time inference dashboard — became the initial production system the team built on. Today I work directly with the founder and one other engineer on nearly all ML architecture and research decisions ahead of launch.

How I work

I learn by building and I prefer evidence over opinions. I'll implement models from scratch rather than take a library for granted, run ablations rather than trust defaults, and sit with results long enough to understand why something worked before shipping it. Most of my time goes to NLP, representation learning, and applied deep learning — and I genuinely enjoy explaining what's going on, in code, in diagrams, or in prose.

What I care about is the feedback loop between theory and implementation: staying close to the math, designing controlled experiments, and letting the results pick the next step. That's how I ended up with LexiMind — a 272M-parameter encoder-decoder transformer I built from scratch (Pre-LN, RMSNorm, T5 relative position bias, FlashAttention, gated-GELU FFN, KV-cache) with explicit FLAN-T5-base weight mapping, then jointly trained on summarization, topic classification, and multi-label emotion detection. The interesting part wasn't the scale, it was diagnosing negative transfer in naive MTL and designing two targeted interventions that lifted emotion-detection sample-averaged F1 from 0.199 to 0.352.
